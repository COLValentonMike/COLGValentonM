The Solar System

Exploring the Wonders of Our Cosmic Neighborhood

Valenton, Mike Aron F.

Article body

The planets in our solar system vary greatly, from the scorching surface of Mercury to the icy landscapes of Neptune.

The sun is the heart of our solar system, providing the energy needed for life on Earth and driving the orbits of all surrounding celestial bodies.

Many moons, orbit planets, with some, like Europa and Titan, showing potential signs of hosting life beneath their icy surfaces.

Asteriods are rocky remnants from the early formation of the solar system, and some have even collided with Earth, shaping its history.

Beyond our own Milky Way, countless galaxies, stretch across the universe, each containing billions of stars and planetary systems.

Scientists continue to discover exoplanets or planets outside our solar system, some of which may have the right conditions to support life.

Advanced spacecraft allow us to explore these distant worlds, sending back incredible data and images that expand our understanding of the cosmos
